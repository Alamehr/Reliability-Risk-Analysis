# Reliability and Risk Analysis in Energy Systems

This repository hosts computational assignments, failure modeling scripts, and quantitative frameworks developed during the graduate-level course **Reliability and Risk Analysis** at Sharif University of Technology. 

The curriculum focuses on the mathematical and probabilistic formulation of failure phenomena rather than standard deterministic metrics. It extensively explores the **Stress-Strength Interference (SSI)** framework to quantify failure probabilities ($P_f = P(D < 0)$) and evaluate design margins using the **Safety Index (SI)**, bridging traditional safety factors with structural risk and reliability acceptance criteria.

## Core Frameworks Covered
- **Physics of Failure Modeling:** Implementation of degradation, fatigue, and overstress mechanisms (including linear, power-law, and exponential growth models).
- **Risk & Barrier Evaluation:** Probabilistic risk assessment ($R = \langle S_i, P_i, C_i \rangle$) utilizing active/passive safety barriers and hierarchy of controls.
- **Statistical Implementations:** Numerical computation of reliability indices ($R(t)$, MTTF, MTBF, availability) and parameter estimation under data uncertainty using advanced statistical toolboxes.
